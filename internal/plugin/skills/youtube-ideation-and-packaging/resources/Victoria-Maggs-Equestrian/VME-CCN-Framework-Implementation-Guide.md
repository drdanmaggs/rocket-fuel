# CCN Framework Implementation Guide - Victoria Maggs Equestrian

## The CCN Framework for Victoria Maggs Equestrian

The CCN (Core, Casual, New) Framework is used as a **content portfolio strategy** — not every video needs to hit all three tiers. Instead, different videos deliberately target different tiers, and the *mix* matters. Each tier has different ambitions and different packaging approaches.

1. **Core**: Dedicated equestrians — specifically amateur showjumpers and equestrian mothers. People very much like Vicky. These videos build depth, trust, and loyalty. You're not chasing views here — you're building the relationship that drives the business.
2. **Casual**: People with a strong interest in horses — maybe they don't own one, but they love the idea of them. Horse lovers, recreational riders, people who follow horse content. These are the 100k+ view swings. Packaging needs to say "you'll enjoy this even if you don't own a horse."
3. **New**: People who aren't in the equestrian space at all, but who have adjacent interests (animals, parenting, speed, competition, fashion) that a well-packaged equestrian video could tap into. **Crucially, "New" does NOT mean "people who don't care about horses" — it means people who don't yet know they'd enjoy a horse-related video.** The entry point isn't horses — it's a universal theme. The horses are what make it novel. These are the 1M+ view moonshots.

### Important: Not Every Video Is a CCN Hit

A video can be a strong Core video, or a strong Core + Casual video, without reaching New at all — and that's fine. The channel needs a healthy mix. Mislabelling a Casual hit as a CCN hit leads to wrong conclusions about what's working and why.

**VME currently has no examples of videos that have reached a genuinely New audience.** The channel's biggest hit (123k views — "I Thought I Knew Horses… Then I Got My First Pony") was a Core + Casual success, not a New audience breakthrough. The title still centres on horses and ponies — someone outside the equestrian space would not have been drawn to click.

To reach New, the concept itself must transcend the niche. Examples of what a New-tier VME video might look like:
- **"Formula 1 Car vs Racehorse"** — entry point is speed and spectacle, not equestrian knowledge
- **Cross-theme content** — parenting, fashion, competition — where the equestrian angle is the flavour, not the subject

## Victoria Maggs Equestrian Audience Segments

### Core Audience 1 - "The Equestrian Mum"

- Female, 35-45 years old
- Married/partnered with 1-3 children (typically one 4-12 years old interested in riding)
- Household income £65,000-£120,000
- Juggling family, work, and horse commitments
- Often managing horses at home rather than at livery
- Solo management of horse care and training
- Struggles with guilt about time spent away from family for riding
- Isolated when local friends give up riding or lose interest
- Seeking meaningful connections with other equestrians

### Core Audience 2 - "The Amateur Showjumper"

- Female, 25-45 years old (core demographic 28-35)
- Professional career that allows flexibility for training
- Income £45,000-£85,000 annually
- Significant portion of disposable income dedicated to horses
- Highly structured routine around training
- Regular training and clinics
- Weekend-focused competition schedule
- Competitive and driven
- Detail-oriented with perfectionist tendencies
- Seeking achievement in competition and personal improvement

### Casual Audience

- Recreational riders not competing seriously
- Horse owners of various disciplines
- People considering horse ownership
- Those interested in equestrian lifestyle
- Parents considering horses for their children
- Countryside/rural lifestyle enthusiasts
- Former riders who maintain interest but not active participation
- Equestrian spectators or fans

### New Audience

- Not currently in the equestrian space — but that doesn't mean they wouldn't enjoy a horse-related video if packaged right
- They don't know yet that they'd enjoy equestrian content — the packaging must give them a reason to click that has nothing to do with horses specifically
- Adjacent interests that could serve as entry points:
  - Animal lovers (not specifically horse people)
  - Parents interested in child development, confidence building, outdoor activities
  - Speed / competition / spectacle enthusiasts
  - Fashion and lifestyle audiences
  - Sports psychology and performance improvement
  - Rural lifestyle curiosity
- The key: the concept must transcend the niche. Horses become the novel vehicle for a universally compelling theme, not the subject that limits the audience

## Implementation Strategy

### Content Balance

**ALL content must primarily serve the Core audience.** The key principle of CCN is that you never sacrifice core value - you simply package it to widen appeal:

- Every video must provide genuine expertise about equestrian topics valuable to your Amateur Showjumper and Equestrian Mum audiences
- Packaging (titles, thumbnails, framing) makes this core content appealing to Casual and New viewers
- The content itself remains evidence-based and relevant to dedicated equestrians

### Title Optimisation

- Create titles that signal value to Core but intrigue Casual and New viewers
- Use universal themes (transformation, expert insights, counterintuitive findings) to widen appeal
- Incorporate curiosity-driving elements that work across knowledge levels
- Avoid excessive technical terminology in titles while still signalling the content to those in the know
- Include time elements where appropriate (quick solutions for busy people)
- Test different title variations to find optimal CTR balance

### Thumbnail Strategy

- Use compelling horse imagery but focus on emotional human reactions
- Show transformational moments or dramatic contrasts
- Incorporate elements that signal expertise without being overly technical
- For parenting content, thoughtfully include children with horses where appropriate
- Keep text minimal (3-5 words maximum) focusing on the most surprising element
- Use contrasting elements (problem vs. solution)
- Ensure all elements are clear on mobile devices

### Content Structure

For each video, regardless of primary target:

1. Open with a hook that appeals to New viewers (surprising fact, counterintuitive statement)
2. Quickly establish credibility and relevance for Core viewers (signal expertise and specific relevance)
3. Throughout video, alternate between:
   - Technical explanations and specific guidance for Core audience
   - Simplified explanations for Casual viewers
   - Broadly interesting narratives or universal principles for New viewers
4. Include your personal journey where relevant (your own experiences add authenticity)
5. End with clear, actionable takeaways relevant to all three segments

### Analytics Tracking

Monitor which videos achieve the best CCN balance by looking at:

- Traffic sources (browse features vs. search vs. suggested)
- New subscriber rates
- Audience retention patterns
- Average view duration
- Click-through rate
- Comment analysis for indicators of viewer background

## Process for Developing CCN-Optimised Content

### Step 1: Start with Core Value

- Identify the primary equestrian principle you want to communicate
- Ensure the content will provide genuine value to your Amateur Showjumper or Equestrian Mum audience
- Map out the key technical points that must be included

### Step 2: Identify Universal Themes

- Extract the aspects of the content with broader human interest
- Look for elements that connect to:
  - Parenting challenges and solutions
  - Time management for busy professionals
  - Performance psychology
  - Confidence building
  - Safety and risk management
  - Personal transformation stories
  - Scientific insights about child development

### Step 3: Craft CCN-Balanced Titles

- Write 15-20 title variations for each video
- Test how each balances appeal across all three audiences
- Select titles that maintain Core value while maximising Casual and New interest
- Consider using title testing tools or getting feedback from various demographics

### Step 4: Design Supporting Thumbnails

- Create thumbnails that complement rather than duplicate the title
- Include visual elements that trigger curiosity
- Ensure the thumbnail works well at small sizes on mobile devices
- Test multiple thumbnail variations for optimal click-through rate

### Step 5: Structure Content for All Audiences

- Open with broadly interesting hooks
- Clearly signal expertise early
- Layer information from broadly accessible to technically detailed
- Include relatable examples without overwhelming new viewers
- End with actionable takeaways for all knowledge levels

By implementing this CCN framework, your YouTube channel can maintain its core focus on equestrian topics for your primary audiences while expanding reach to broader viewer segments, ultimately growing your influence and business potential.
