# CCN Framework (Core, Casual, New)

## Description

A framework for evaluating the potential reach and appeal of YouTube video ideas by ensuring they engage three categories of viewers: core, casual, and new. The goal is to expand viewership while maintaining relevance to a channel's existing audience.

## Steps / Components

1. **Core** - These are the most dedicated fans or viewers within your niche. The video should provide value to them.
2. **Casual** - Viewers who dip in and out of your niche, or who have some level of interest but aren't deeply invested. The video should still interest this broader segment.
3. **New** - People who are outside the niche or unfamiliar with your content entirely. The video should have an angle, hook, or packaging that can catch their attention and encourage a click.

## Key Examples

- **Permaculture Channel (Andrew Millison)**:
  - Niche: Horticulture / Permaculture
  - Viral Video: _"How the UN is Holding Back the Sahara Desert"_ - 12 million views in 4 months.
  - Analysis: While his niche is highly specific, the video appealed to:
    - Core (permaculture enthusiasts)
    - Casual (people interested in sustainability or development)
    - New (general interest due to the hook involving the UN and Sahara Desert)

- **Woodworking Channel (731 Woodworks)**:
  - Viral formats included:
    - _"99% of Woodworkers Get This Wrong"_
    - _"This Tool is Now Illegal in Seven and a Half States"_
  - Analysis: These titles appeal to:
    - Core (experienced woodworkers)
    - Casual (DIY hobbyists)
    - New (people intrigued by controversy or legality angle)

- **Noah Kagan**:
  - Initially created narrow business content (e.g., "How to use Slack").
  - Shifted to broader ideas like "Asking millionaires for advice".
  - Analysis: Broader appeal brought in new and casual viewers while still retaining his entrepreneurial core audience.

## Definitions

- **CCN**: A framework for identifying whether a video idea appeals to:
  - **Core** - deeply invested audience in the niche
  - **Casual** - loosely connected audience
  - **New** - those outside the niche but potentially interested through broader themes

## Principles / Best Practices

- Ask of every video idea: "Can a core, casual, and new viewer all click this video and enjoy it?"
- Avoid focusing too narrowly on just the core audience; it limits growth.
- Avoid going too broad and losing connection with your core audience.
- Expand niche appeal by identifying "upstream" interests - larger categories your topic fits within.
- Use existing top-performing videos in your niche to estimate TAM (Total Addressable Market) and spot viewership benchmarks.
- Apply the CCN framework in tandem with novelty and title/thumbnail clarity for viral potential.
