# Viral Video Traits

## Description

A set of characteristics commonly found in viral videos across different niches.

## Steps / Components

1. Core Casual New (CCN) Fit - the idea should appeal to core viewers, casual viewers, and new viewers
2. Unique novelty value - something that makes the video feel like its own thing, not just another common idea
3. Easy to convey in title and thumbnail - must be simple and clear to understand quickly
4. Excitement from others - people, including peers or others in the niche, should find the idea genuinely exciting

## Key Examples

- Andrew Millison's video: _How the UN is Holding Back the Sahara Desert_ - shows novelty, simplicity in packaging, and broad appeal
- 731 Woodworks videos - use of viral formats like "testing woodworking hacks," "99% of woodworkers get this thing wrong," "this tool is now illegal," etc.

## Definitions

- **Core Casual New (CCN)**: A framework for evaluating if a video appeals to:
  - Core viewers who are deeply engaged in the niche
  - Casual viewers who occasionally dip in and out of the niche
  - New viewers who are unfamiliar but might click due to interesting packaging

## Principles / Best Practices

- Apply the CCN filter to every video idea
- Look at examples in your niche and ask if they appeal to all three CCN audiences
- Simplicity and novelty are key drivers of viral potential

## Phrases / Templates

- "Core Casual New"
- "Something unique, something novel"
- "Easy to convey in title and thumbnail"
- "Does that make you excited?"
