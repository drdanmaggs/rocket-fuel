# Expert Profile: Jay Clouse

## Background & Credibility

Jay Clouse is a YouTube strategist who grew his channel to 100,000 subscribers in just over two years. He hosts an interview-style show with his producer Connor, focusing on creator economy topics. Their success demonstrates practical application of their strategies, with videos reaching millions of views (e.g., their Jenny Hoops video hit 3.5 million views).

## Core Philosophy

Packaging (titles, thumbnails, hooks) is the exponential multiplier of YouTube success, while content quality and proper pre-production provide the foundation. Success comes from making fewer, higher-quality videos rather than maintaining frequent but mediocre output.

## Key Frameworks & Models

### Core-Casual-New Audience Funnel

- **Core Audience**: Dedicated viewers who watch most content
- **Casual Audience**: Intermittent viewers who watch selected content
- **New Audience**: First-time viewers who need to be converted to casual/core
- **Application**: All content decisions should consider which audience segment is being targeted and how to move viewers down the funnel from new to casual to core
- **Click Triggers**: Implementing elements in packaging that target specific psychological triggers that motivate clicks

### Packaging Exponential Impact Model

- **Premise**: Small improvements in packaging (titles, thumbnails) create exponential rather than linear improvements in performance
- **Components**:
  - Title optimisation (10% improvement can yield 2-10x more views)
  - Thumbnail design (should work alongside title)
  - Hook creation (first 30-60 seconds)
- **Application**: Invest disproportionate time in packaging elements compared to content production

### Pre-Production Topic-First Framework

- **Premise**: Frame interviews around specific topics rather than guest identity
- **Components**:
  - Research audience needs through comments and platform feedback
  - Identify specialist topics viewers want to learn about
  - Match guests to topics rather than topics to guests
  - Prepare episode structure before recording
- **Application**: This approach makes packaging easier and more effective while ensuring content meets audience needs

### Candy-Vegetables Content Balance

- **Concept**: People want candy (entertainment) but need vegetables (education)
- **Application**: Package educational content as entertaining/easy to consume
- **Techniques**:
  - Use fifth-grade reading level for titles and intros
  - Focus on outcomes rather than processes in packaging
  - Ease viewers into complex topics gradually
  - Start with light, accessible content before diving into depth

### The Guest as Teacher Model

- **Premise**: Treat guests as subject matter experts rather than celebrities
- **Components**:
  - Package around what guests can teach rather than who they are
  - Create workshops through people rather than profiles of people
  - Pre-plan specific teaching points rather than general conversation topics
- **Application**: Results in more evergreen, unique content compared to personality-driven interviews

## Strategic Approaches

### Content Strategy

- **Quality over Quantity**: Fewer, better videos outperform more, worse videos
- **Audience Fit**: Content should align with intended audience rather than chasing viral success
- **Long-form Value**: Longer content (30+ minutes) can provide better watch time metrics even with lower retention percentage
- **Schedule Flexibility**: YouTube is less schedule-dependent than commonly believed; consistency in quality matters more than publishing calendar

### Audience Building

- **Target Similar Audiences**: Collaborate with creators who have similar (not just larger) audiences
- **Watchtime Prioritisation**: Focus on total watch minutes rather than view count
- **End Screen Strategy**: Actively guide viewers to other videos to increase session time
- **Avoid Audience Capture**: Resist the temptation to chase views by narrowing content scope
- **Content Resuscitation**: Use end screens to revive older content by connecting it to newer successful videos

### Analytics & Optimisation

- **CTR Prioritisation**: For new creators, focus first on improving click-through rate before retention
- **First-Minute Retention**: Improving retention in the first 30-60 seconds creates exponential improvement across the entire video
- **Device Type Analysis**: Understanding viewing patterns across devices (mobile, desktop, TV) to optimise format
- **Viral Hangover Management**: Plan for algorithm adjustment period after viral success

## Actionable Tactics

### Pre-Production

- Create 25-40 title variations before selecting final title
- Test titles against click triggers identified by experts like Jake Thomas
- Research audience needs through comments and topic performance
- Plan episode structure before recording rather than after
- Avoid asking guests to introduce themselves

### Intro/Hook Creation

- First 30 seconds is the highest leverage point for retention
- Use "sucker punch" hook clips with zero context or explanation
- Include the promise from packaging (title/thumbnail) in the first 30 seconds
- Host should introduce the guest rather than asking them to introduce themselves
- Explain exactly what the episode will cover in the intro

### Production

- For remote interviews, look directly at camera rather than off-screen
- Use captions for mobile viewers (50% of audience)
- Consider mobile viewing experience in all decisions
- Don't try to mimic in-studio feel for remote recordings

### Packaging

- Write titles at fifth-grade reading level (standard journalism practice)
- Frame benefits as outcomes rather than processes (e.g., "earn £10,000 per month" vs "improve monetisation")
- Create multiple thumbnail variations
- Test title variations rigorously
- Package builds the promise that content must deliver

### End Screen Strategy

- Create direct calls-to-action for next videos to watch
- Bridge current video content to recommended next video
- Don't telegraph the ending, transition directly into recommendation
- Use end screens to resurface older, underperforming content
